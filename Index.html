Nancy Wake

Nancy Wake aka 'White Mouse' Secret Agent

Timeline of Nancy Wake's Life

Pre-War

  • 1912 - born in Roseneath, Wellington, New Zealand.
  • 1914 - Family moved to Sydney, Australia
  • 1918 - Ran away from home and worked as a nurse
  • 1932 - Moved to Europe and traveleed around working as a Journalist
  • 1932 - Met French Industralist Henri Emdon Fiocca
  • 1939 - Married Henri Fiocca

War

  • 1940-42 - Joined the French Resistance as a courier. Nazi's nicknamed her the 'White Mouse'
  • 1943 - Fled France to England and convinced the English to train her as a professional spy. She joined the French Section of the Special Operations Executive. During this time her husband was captured and tortured by the Nazi's as he would not betray her. He died. She didn't learn of this until after the war ended.
  • 1944 - Parachuted into the Auvergne becoming a liason between London and the local maquis group. She was involved in allocating arms and equipment, minding the group's finances, attacks on bridges, railway lines and German Convoys. She participated in a raid on Gestapo Headquarters in Montlucon which killed 39 Germans.
  • 1944 - Escaping the Germans and Cycling 500km in 72 hours. Wake and her teaam were escaping the Germans when the radio operator realised that he had left his codes behind. Wake borrown a bike, cycled to Chateauroux contacted London and then cycled back to her team in Saint-Satin, 500km in 72 hours! Throughtout this time continued to participate in further raids.
  • 1944 - September - Returned to Great Britain

Post-War

  • 1945 - July - Awareded the George Medal, the United States Medal of Freedom, the Medaille de la Resistance, and three times the Croix de Guerre.
  • 1949 - Stood as a Liberal Candidate in the Australian Federal edlction for hte Sydney seat of Barton.
  • 1951 - Moved back to England and worded as an intelligence officer in the department of the Assistant Chief of the Air Staff at the Air Ministry in Whitehall.
  • 1957 - Resigned and married RAF officer John Forward
  • 1960's - moved back to Australia with her husband.
  • 1966 - Endorsed as a liberal candidate for the Sydney seat of Kingsford Smith, again was unsuccessful.
  • 1970 - Was appointed a Chevalier of the Legion of Honour.
  • 1985 - Retired to Port Macquarie and published her autobiography, 'The White Mouse,' becoming a bestseller and has been reprinted many times.
  • 1988 - Promoted to Officer of the Legion of Honour.
  • 1997 - Her husband died at Port Macquarie on the 19th of August. The couple had no children. She then sold her medals to fund herself.
  • 2001 - Moved back to the England. She became residen that the Stafford Hotel in St. James Place. She could be found in the mornings sipping her first Gin and Tonic. She celebrated her 90th birthday at the Hotel.
  • >2003 - Moved to the Royal Star and Garter Home for the Disabled Ex-Service Men and Women, in Richmond, London. She stayed there until her Death.
  • 2004 - Was made a Companion of the Order of Australia.
  • 2006 - the Royal New Zealand Returned and Services' Association awarded her their highest honour, the RSA Badge in Gold. Her medals are on display in the Second War Gallery at the Australian War Memorial Museum in Canberra.
  • 2010 - A 'Heritage Pylon' paying tripbut to Wake was unveiled on Oriental Parade in Wellington, New Zealand near her place of birth.
  • 2011 - Died on the 7th of August in the evening aged 98. She had admitted to hospital with a chest infection.
  • 2013 - Her ashes were scattered near the village of Verneix, this is near Montlucon which is where she had requested her ashes be scattered.

Nancy Wake can be summed up with these words from her fellow resistance officer, Henri Tardivat.
